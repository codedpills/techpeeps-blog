When Ada Okonkwo talks about the systems she builds, she keeps returning to a
single image: a family spread across three continents, trying to move money home
on a Sunday night. "The textbooks assume everyone shares a timezone and a
currency," she said. "Most of the world doesn't."

## Designing for the edges

For Ada, the interesting problems live at the margins — the corridors where
banks are thin and trust is expensive. She described spending months simply
listening before writing a line of code, learning how money actually flows
through informal networks.

The result, she explained, was infrastructure that treated those edge cases as
the main case. "If it works for the hardest corridor," she said, "it works for
the easy ones for free."

## What the diaspora taught her

The throughline of the conversation was patience: the willingness to sit with a
problem until its real shape emerges. It's a quietly radical stance in an
industry that prizes speed.
